Abstract

The autonomous disassembly of industrial products is a critical bottleneck in smart recycling, primarily due to the difficulty of localizing fasteners on complex, non-planar surfaces from degraded or incomplete visual data. This paper presents a high-precision 3D vision-based metrology framework that isolates and estimates the 6D pose of small fasteners on irregular geometries without relying on structural computer-aided design (CAD) information. The core of the methodology is an advanced 3D point cloud segmentation backend driven by a Superpoint Transformer, which is explicitly optimized to handle severe scan incompleteness caused by line-of-sight constraints, self-occlusions, and shadows. Segmented point clusters are automatically registered against standard geometric templates using a coarse-to-fine pipeline to extract precise 3D center positions and axial surface normals. Experimental results demonstrate that the Superpoint Transformer achieves a superior complete screw segmentation rate of 89.0%, maintaining high robustness even under highly restricted (30%) surface scan coverages. Physical experiments conducted on a 3D printed workpiece and a MakerBot 3D printer demonstrate that the proposed framework delivers sub-millimeter positioning accuracy with a 90% success rate in screw removal on complex, curved, and inclined surfaces. Combined with online localization and a tactile mating protocol, the framework, even with imperfect scans, reliably guides a robotic screwdriver to align, engage, and extract screws across horizontal, inclined, curved, and recessed surfaces.
