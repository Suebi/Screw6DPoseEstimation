Novel Methodology for 3D Vision-Guided Robotic Fastener Removal

Abstract

Fastener removal from complex geometries, such as inclined, recessed, and curved surfaces, remains challenging and often suffers from low success rates due to the difficulty of accurately estimating the six-degree-of-freedom (6-DoF) fastener pose, including the fastener center position and axial orientation relative to the mounting surface. In addition, successful fastener removal requires precise identification of the fastener type, size, and rotational alignment angle. These requirements make autonomous fastener removal a challenging task. Most existing studies rely on computer-aided design (CAD) models to obtain this information and guide robotic fastener removal. However, accurate CAD models of the target assembly are not always available in practice. To address these challenges, this paper proposes a novel vision-guided robotic fastener removal methodology that integrates 3D sensing, fastener pose estimation, surface-normal estimation, and vision-guided robotic control. The proposed methodology consists of an offline phase and an online phase. In the offline phase, deep learning-based point cloud analytics are developed to extract the fastener center position and axial-normal direction from structured light 3D scans. A deep learning-based segmentation network is trained to segment fasteners from the point cloud, and the segmented screws fastener points are then fed into a classification network to identify the fastener type and size. The 6-DoF pose of each fastener is obtained by matching the segmented point cloud with the CAD model of the corresponding fastener, which is often available from online repositories. In the online phase, an eye-in-hand RGB-D camera is used to transform the estimated 6-DoF fastener pose into the robot coordinate frame, enabling the robot to perform bit insertion and fastener removal. Extensive experiments rigorously quantify the sensing errors in fastener center-position estimation and normal-direction estimation. Final fastener removal tests on more than 800 fasteners of different types, including hex-head, Phillips, and hex-socket fasteners, and sizes achieved a 91% success rate, outperforming state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods.
